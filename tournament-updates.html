Email 1 (10/23/23) – Invitational Announcement

Hello everyone!

We're excited to announce the inaugural Yellow Jacket Invitational at Georgia Tech! The tournament will take place on February 3rd, 2024 on-campus at Georgia Tech. The tournament will be open to 50 Division C teams from across the country.

If you're interested in volunteering as an event supervisor, please fill out the form here. For general volunteers, please fill out the form here.

If you have any connections with a high school (not limited to Georgia teams) who you think might be interested in participating in this event, you can direct them to sign up at the application here

Note that this tournament is not the state tournament; we'll be announcing volunteer registration for the state tournament in the near future.

For more information, please visit https://sciolygatech.org or email us at scioly.gatech@gmail.com.

Email 5 (1/29/24 – Coach Update #4)

Dear coaches,

Happy tournament week! This email contains a lot of logistical information regarding the tournament this Saturday, so please read through it carefully and share necessary information with your students.

Tournament Communiques. For previous tournament emails, please visit the document here. You can also find more information on our website, including registered teams and the tournament information packet. If you are printing maps/schedules, note that these documents are subject to change up until February 1st. Therefore, it is recommended that you use the hyperlinks on page 4 of the tournament information packet to access these documents. Please read through this packet for a source for the most updated information.

Homerooms. Homerooms can be found in the tournament information packet. All homerooms should unlock by 7:30am and should remain unlocked until 6pm. If your homeroom has not been unlocked by this time please come to tournament headquarters (CULC 144).

Check-In. Check-in for teams will be in CULC 144 beginning at 7:20am. Any teams that have not paid prior to the tournament date should be ready to pay the registration fee ($125/team) plus late fee ($25/team) at check-in. The room is a large lecture hall, and we will be setting up near the front of the room. To avoid congestion, please send only one head coach into the room for check-in.

Tournament Headquarters. Tournament HQ will be from 7:20am to 4:00pm in CULC 144. After 4:00pm, please go to East Architecture 123 for any questions and scoring inquiries.

Appeals. Appeals can only be made by the head coach on file. Students cannot submit appeals. The procedure will be as follows:

  1. Ask your student to attempt to resolve the appeal with the head event supervisor. If not possible, then;
  2. The head coach will come to tournament HQ. We will document the appeal and resolve it with our scoring team and the event supervisor.

Awards. This year we’re going to hold awards concurrently in the two largest lecture halls (144 and 152) in Clough Undergraduate Learning Commons. Schools will be assigned to one of the rooms, both of which will have medals, announcers, and guest speakers running simultaneously. We will be checking at the door to ensure that you go to the correct awards room, and we’ll be posting a full list outside the awards rooms. The tournament information document will be updated with the awards seating arrangement closer to the tournament date; we are still finalizing a couple of details.

Event Sign-Up. Event sign-up has been closed as of January 26th. If you are one of the four teams who did not complete the sign-up, don’t worry. In general, your team will be able to compete for any of the self sign-up events in any of the blocks, but will be given priority behind teams who registered for that block. Please ask your students to communicate with the event supervisors during the day of the competition to determine when the best time to compete is.

Parking. Parking and drop-off info will be further clarified in an email sent out later this week.

Payment Reminder. Payment statuses are updated here for all checks that have arrived and been received by us. If your check is not able to be cashed for any reason, we will reach out to you individually.

Flight Room Specifications. Flight will be held in the Klaus Advanced Computing Building Atrium (Research Wing). Its room dimensions are 11m x 13m x 12.5m (36.1’ x 42.7’ x 41.0’). Detailed information is provided in the tournament information packet.

Food

We are not able to provide food on the day of the tournament, but there are many food options both on and off campus. More information about on-campus food vendors can be found in the tournament information packet.

Makerspace Tours. As the premiere technical/engineering school in the southeast, Georgia Tech has provided an abundance of resources into developing makerspaces. All makerspaces are student-run and promote invention, innovation, and creativity for all disciplines across the college campus.

Registration is limited for each makerspace tour and will open on Tuesday, January 30 at 11 AM EST. At that time, sign-ups will be made available on our website. All makerspace tours are first come, first serve. Due to the limited availability, all registrants are expected to attend their respective tours. For more information on makerspaces click on the link above.

Scavenger Hunts. To honor our amazing mascot (our celebrity) Buzz, we will be hiding 8 pictures of him across campus in the locations where events are held! We will be sharing hints to our Instagram story throughout the competition day (@gtscioly). If a student finds 5 out of the 8 pictures of Buzz  they'll have a chance at winning one of our very amazing prizes! To stay in touch with the scavenger hunt and some of the other fun things we have planned during the invitational, tell your team members to follow us on Instagram @gtscioly.

We hope to see you on campus soon!

Best,

Allen Chang
